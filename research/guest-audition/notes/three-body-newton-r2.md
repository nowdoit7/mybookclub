  https://www.newtonproject.ox.ac.uk/view/texts/normalized/NATP00056
  - Basis used: distinguish relationships supported by phenomena from

  https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/newton-philosophy/
  - Basis used: test extrapolations against contrary phenomena and revise broad
    conclusions when a concrete exception requires it.

  https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/newton-principia/
  - Basis used: limit a conclusion to what the available observations warrant.

These sources justify the three recurring reasoning moves only. The case does
not reproduce Newton's wording, reconstruct his conversational voice, or claim
that he read the modern trilogy.

## Limits and red-team risks

- Newton's methods concern early-modern natural philosophy, not literary
  criticism or democratic accountability. Their use here is an explicit
  imaginative extrapolation.
- The transcript is generated discussion material, not a complete account of
  the trilogy or its author's intent. The challenge is limited to evidence the
  existing table raised.
- Formulaic references to hypotheses, laws, gravity, optics, apples, or proof
  would turn the fingerprint into costume. They are explicitly forbidden.
- The newly composed cues must not become catchphrases: no more than one cue may
  appear in a turn, and a production session should rotate or omit them.
- Blind comparison can test contribution and naturalness, but historical
  accuracy, legal safety, and user response to a famous name still require
  separate review.
