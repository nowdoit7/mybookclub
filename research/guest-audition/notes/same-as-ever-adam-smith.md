# Same as Ever × Adam Smith — audition notes

## Scope

This case is a research-only, Korean-language blind A/B comparison. It replaces
one contextual reader with either a regular contextual baseline or an imagined
reader whose reasoning is constrained by documented ideas from Adam Smith. The
book evidence comes only from the two completed `불변의 법칙` session artifacts
listed in the case JSON; no source-book passage or outside summary was used.

The sample containing the imagined guest was randomly assigned with PowerShell
`Get-Random -InputObject @('sample-a','sample-b')`. The result was `sample-a`.
Both samples preserve the same topic, turn count, speaker order, moderator text,
user text, and other-reader text. `Reader X` is the only changed contribution.

## Documented portrayal basis

1. **Prudence as security and foresight.** In Part VI of *The Theory of Moral
   Sentiments*, Smith describes prudence as care for one's health, fortune,
   standing, and reputation. He gives security priority over exposing those
   goods to needless hazard, and associates prudence with foresight, genuine
   knowledge, steady industry, frugality, and circumspection. This supports a
   cautious reader attentive to downside and durability, not a modern portfolio
   theorist.
   Source: https://www.adamsmithworks.org/documents/section-i-of-the-character-of-the-individual-so-far-as-it-affects-his-own-happiness-or-of-prudence

2. **Prudence, justice, and the standpoint of others.** Smith distinguishes
   concern for one's own welfare, which recommends prudence, from justice and
   beneficence toward other people. He also makes the imagined impartial
   spectator central to directing these virtues. The audition therefore lets
   Reader X ask whether a risk-control rule is acceptable to the people who bear
   its harms, rather than treating private caution as sufficient.
   Source: https://www.adamsmithworks.org/documents/conclusion-of-the-sixth-part

3. **Institutions protect against injury but are historically situated.** The
   University of Glasgow's account of Smith's jurisprudence describes government
   as maintaining justice and protecting person, property, reputation, and
   relationships, while emphasizing that legal and governmental institutions
   differ with social conditions. This supports translating moral restraint into
   procedures without claiming Smith supplied a timeless veto-system blueprint.
   Source: https://www.gla.ac.uk/explore/adamsmith300/explorelearn/ideas/keyworks/lecturesonjurisprudence/

4. **Moral judgment has limits.** The Stanford Encyclopedia explains Smith's
   impartial-spectator account and notes scholarly objections: it does not yield
   a mechanical decision procedure and may inherit the biases of its society.
   Reader X therefore offers a contestable perspective and remains answerable to
   other participants rather than speaking with historical authority.
   Source: https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/smith-moral-political/

## Book evidence carried into the case

- Both completed sessions treated safety margin as capacity to survive or
  recover when prediction fails.
- The first session debated Arkhipov, heroic restraint, agreement procedures,
  veto power, and the risk of decision paralysis.
- The second session distinguished external shocks from risks whose probability
  can be reduced, while refusing to erase responsibility by calling everything
  luck.
- The returning-aircraft example supplied a shared warning against evaluating a
  system only from visible survivors.

These are summaries of statements made at the two tables. They are not asserted
as verbatim content of Housel's book.

## Limitations and red-team cautions

- Smith died in 1790. The imagined guest must never say or imply that he read
  *Same as Ever*, knew Arkhipov, studied modern organizations, or endorsed a
  particular contemporary risk-management method.
- The source on prudence concerns a moral virtue, not a formal theory of
  probability, tail risk, resilient engineering, or safety margins. All links to
  those topics are analogies made inside this audition.
- Smith's impartial spectator is not an infallible neutral algorithm. It can be
  underdetermined and socially biased, so it cannot settle the group's dispute
  by authority.
- The jurisprudence material does not establish that Smith would support this
  case's proposed veto and review procedures. The portrayal may ask how private
  restraint becomes shared security, but it must leave the concrete design open.
- No wording in the candidate turns is presented as a historical quotation.
  The prose is newly composed and should remain labeled as an imagined reading.
- This single title cannot establish repeat resistance. Adam Smith must also be
  tested on unrelated books and against other candidates before product use.

## Blind-evaluation handoff

Evaluators should receive the case JSON without `guestCandidate`,
`guestSampleId`, this note, or the source list. They should score the two samples
only after confirming that the fixed non-candidate turns and ordering match.
The guest mapping may be disclosed solely for the subsequent historical-safety
and authority-balance review.
